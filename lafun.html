How to Make LAFUN

Cassava Lafun is a traditional fermented cassava product widely used in Nigerian cuisine. It is made by peeling, fermenting, drying, and milling cassava into a fine flour. Lafun is typically cooked into a smooth, stretchy paste and served with soups like Egusi, Ogbono, or Okra. Here is a step-by-step guide to making cassava lafun at home.

Steps to Process Cassava Lafun

Phase 1: Peeling and Fermentation

  • Peel the cassava: Remove the thick brown skin and the thin inner layer to expose the white cassava.
  • Wash thoroughly: Rinse the peeled cassava to remove dirt and debris.
  • Ferment: Place the peeled cassava in clean water and leave to ferment for 3–5 days to develop sour flavor.

Phase 2: Drying

  • Drain and slice: Remove fermented cassava from water and slice into smaller pieces.
  • Sun-dry or oven-dry: Dry the fermented cassava until completely moisture-free to prevent spoilage.

Phase 3: Milling into Lafun

  • Grind dried cassava: Use a mechanical or manual mill to convert dried cassava into fine Lafun flour.
  • Cook into Lafun: Boil water and gradually stir in Lafun flour. Cook while stirring until smooth, stretchy, and lump-free.
  • Serve: Mold the cooked Lafun into balls and serve hot with your favorite Nigerian soup.
