How to Make Cassava Tapioca

Cassava Tapioca is a popular snack and dessert made from cassava starch. It can be cooked into a soft, chewy pudding or used in other recipes. Tapioca is naturally gluten-free and provides a smooth texture. Below is a step-by-step guide on how to prepare cassava tapioca from fresh cassava.

Steps to Process Cassava Tapioca

Phase 1: Extracting Starch from Cassava

  • Peel and wash cassava: Remove the brown outer skin and thin inner layer. Rinse thoroughly.
  • Grate cassava: Use a manual or mechanical grater to create a fine mash of the cassava.
  • Extract starch: Place the grated cassava in a muslin cloth or sieve, add water, and squeeze to extract the starchy liquid.

Phase 2: Settling and Drying the Starch

  • Settle the starch: Pour the extracted liquid into a bowl and let it settle for several hours. The starch will sink to the bottom.
  • Remove water: Carefully pour off the clear water, leaving wet starch behind.
  • Dry the starch: Spread the wet starch on a tray and sun-dry or oven-dry until completely dry.

Phase 3: Cooking Tapioca

  • Boil water: Bring water or milk to a boil, depending on your recipe.
  • Add cassava starch: Gradually stir the dried cassava starch or tapioca pearls into boiling water.
  • Cook until translucent: Stir continuously until the tapioca becomes soft, chewy, and translucent.
  • Serve: Optionally add sugar, coconut milk, or other flavors. Serve warm as pudding or dessert.
