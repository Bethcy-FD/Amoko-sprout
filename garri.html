How to Make GARRI from Cassava

Garri is a popular West African staple made from fermented cassava. It can be eaten as a snack or prepared into eba to accompany soups like Egusi, Ogbono, and Okra. Making garri preserves cassava for longer periods and enhances its digestibility and taste. Below, we guide you step by step through the traditional process of making cassava garri.

Steps to Process Cassava into Garri

Phase 1: Peeling and Fermentation

  • Peel and wash the cassava: Cut off the thick outer brown skin and the thin inner layer to reveal the white flesh. Rinse thoroughly.
  • Soak the cassava: Cut into small chunks and place in a large container. Cover with water and allow to ferment for 3–5 days.
  • Check for fermentation: Properly fermented cassava becomes soft and gives off a sour, pungent aroma.

Phase 2: Grating and Pressing

  • Grate the cassava: Use a manual or mechanical grater to turn the fermented cassava into fine mash.
  • Press out excess water: Place the grated cassava in a press bag or sack and apply weight to squeeze out moisture.
  • Sieving: Sieve the pressed mash to remove coarse fibers, leaving a finer texture ready for roasting.

Phase 3: Roasting the Garri

  • Heat the pan: Use a large shallow pan over medium heat. Ensure the pan is dry before adding cassava mash.
  • Roast gradually: Pour small amounts of cassava mash into the pan. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
  • Achieve desired dryness: Roast until granules are dry, slightly crispy, and golden. Cool completely before storage.
