Weed Control Management & Tips

Keeping weeds under control is essential for maintaining healthy plants, productive crops, and a clean landscape. Effective weed management protects your soil, saves time, and supports stronger plant growth.

What Are Weeds?

Weeds are plants growing where they are not wanted. Many different types of weeds occur in cassava farms and cause considerable losses to the farmer. This is because weeds compete with the cassava crop for nutrients, sunlight, and space. Weeds may harbor pests and diseases or physically injure cassava plants and storage roots.

What Is Weed Control?

Weed control involves reducing or eliminating unwanted plants that compete with your crops or landscape for sunlight, water, and nutrients. Proper management helps maintain a clean, healthy growing environment and improves overall plant performance.

Weed Control Methods

Preventive Measures

  • Use clean, weed-free soil and manure.
  • Install weed barriers and landscape fabric.
  • Maintain proper plant spacing.

Mechanical & Physical Techniques

  • Hand weeding or digging (best after rainfall).
  • Flame weeding for non-flammable areas.
  • Mow tall weeds before they seed.

Cultural Techniques

  • Crop rotation for farm and field management.
  • Plant cover crops such as clover or rye.
  • Use drip irrigation to limit water access for weeds.

Chemical Techniques (Use Carefully)

  • Follow all label directions and safety guidelines.
  • Apply during calm, dry weather.
  • Spot-treat weeds instead of spraying large areas.

Examples of Herbicides Used in Cassava Weeds

  • Roundup by Monsanto
  • Touchdown Forte by Syngenta
  • Primextra Gold by Syngenta
