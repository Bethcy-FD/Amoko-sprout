How to Make Cassava Chips

Cassava chips are a crunchy snack made from fresh cassava tubers.
They can be enjoyed on their own or served with dips.
This process involves peeling, slicing, soaking, frying, and seasoning to produce golden, crispy chips.

Steps to Process Cassava Chips

  • 1. Peel the cassava: Remove the thick brown skin and thin inner layer to reveal the white flesh.
  • 2. Slice the cassava: Cut the peeled tubers into thin, even slices for uniform frying.
  • 3. Soak the slices: Optional: soak in salted water for 30–60 minutes to reduce starch and improve crispiness.
  • 4. Drain and dry: Pat slices dry with a clean cloth or air-dry to remove excess moisture.
  • 5. Fry the slices: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan and fry slices until golden and crispy.
  • 6. Season and store: Drain excess oil, sprinkle with salt or seasoning, and allow to cool before storing in an airtight container.
